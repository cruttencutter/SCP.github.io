This website was created to provide tips for working with children with disabilities, more specifically working as a volunteer. I have done research and taken information from multiple sources to create a curated list with some helpful tips. You can view the tips below.







Tips for Volunteering with Children with Diabilities


1. Celebrate small accomplishments--Try to remember that even small accomplishments are still accomplishments that should be celebrated!

2. Manage your expectations--Don't push the child too hard if they're having trouble understanding something or doing something.

3. Be flexible--Use different methods to help children learn. All children are different and require different responses to learn efficiently.

4. Communicate--Communicate clearly with the child to make sure you understand each other. Ask questions, have conversations, etc.

5. Listen--Listen to what the child is saying and ask for clarification if necessary. This will help you understand the child better and create more fun conversations!

6. Be firm--Don't be afraid to be firm if the child refuses to participate. If they refuse to listen to you, follow tip 11.

7. Offer alternative solutions--If the child doesn't understand something or isn't able to do it, offer alternative solutions to the problem.

8. Maintain their focus--Try to keep the child's focus on the activity and make lots of eye contact when you're talking to them.

9. Interact with them--Introduce yourself and get to know them. Explain your relationship with the child and the activity you'll be doing together.

10. Break down instructions--Use simple instructions to help break it down into more manageable tasks.

11. Ask for help if needed--Don't be afraid to ask for help from the people in charge or even the parents. They want to help you!

12. Stay calm, collected, and positive--Don't become too frustrated or stressed out. Try to keep a positive attitude and do your best.

13. HAVE FUN!--Try to make sure both you and the child are having a fun time! Enjoy yourself!
